How this leg works

The race starts here. BD owns the relationship before there's a project to run: hunting opportunities, building trust, and converting conversations into awarded work. This is the prospecting leg: outreach, capabilities presentations, lead tracking, and the groundwork that makes the pitch possible.

When a project is won, BD hands the baton and the leg is complete. But the finish line is also the start line. Every case study, every portfolio win, every piece of PR that comes out of Leg 4 feeds directly back into BD's ability to open the next door. The loop is intentional.

The BD team's role
  • Identifies and develops new opportunities across Agency, Brand / ENT, and IP Originals
  • Manages outreach, relationship building, and pipeline tracking
  • Leads capabilities presentations and pitches in partnership with EPs and ECD
  • Owns the client relationship until award — then formally hands off to the Production EP
  • Leverages delivered work and marketing materials to generate future pipeline
Team on this leg
BD Team & PR / Marketing Core
  • Drives all outreach, lead generation, and pipeline development
BD EPs Core
  • Lead the bid and client relationship through to award
ECD ~80% of jobs
  • Involved in capabilities presentations and creative positioning
Most marketing materials that fuel this leg are created between Legs 1 and 4 — not pulled together at the last minute. The better Leg 4 performs, the stronger Leg 1 becomes.
Leg 2 · Baton Pass #1
Project Intake / Scoping

Close ✕
How this leg works

This stage is when there is an active opportunity to bid or pitch a project. A job name and number is made, the incoming opportunity email has been sent and we are ready to move forward.

A project can live and end here with a pitch submission. However, if the project is moving forward with bidding, the budget set on this leg is the budget lived with for the rest of the race. If it's wrong here, it's wrong everywhere. This is the most consequential moment for financial health.

Depending on the stage of the project, there is a variety of involvement from the key teammates.

The BD EP's role
  • Leads or supports the bid: scoping, pricing, and presenting to the client
  • Ensures the budget is properly set before handoff — if it's wrong at award, it's wrong forever
  • Transitions the client relationship cleanly to the Production EP at award
  • Stays available as a senior resource if the relationship needs attention later in the race
The Production EP's role
  • Takes full ownership of the client relationship at award
  • Leads or oversees the scoping, brief, and treatment process
  • Validates that the budget is properly set before the project moves forward
  • Aligns with ECD on creative approach and feasibility
  • Sets the client up for a smooth handoff into Leg 3
The HOP's role
  • Receives the internal handoff and immediately owns execution: system setup, team assignment, resourcing
  • Initializes the project in Timmy and all studio systems
  • Builds the resourcing plan and identifies freelance needs
  • Schedules internal kickoff and ensures all teams are aligned before production begins
  • Passes the baton to Producers + Creative once the project is fully set up and running
Team on this leg
BD EP Handoff
  • Transitions the client and context to the Production EP — then steps back
Production EP Core
  • Takes client ownership, leads scoping and bid, sets the creative direction
ECD ~90% of jobs
  • Involved in creative approach, treatment development, and pitch
HOP Overlap moment
  • Flags resourcing capacity for pipeline planning; owns all internal setup, resourcing, job planning, and financial initialization
Producer Situational
  • Brought in on certain project types during intake and planning; contributes to internal setup and resourcing if necessary
This is the most consequential handoff on the track. A clean, documented baton pass — not a drift — sets the tone for everything that follows. Budget wrong here means budget wrong everywhere.
Leg 3 · Baton Pass #2
Production

Close ✕
How this leg works

BD hands off the baton at award. The BD EP shifts from hunter to steward — their active leg is done. The Production EP takes full ownership of the client from this moment through delivery, and the HOP takes the operational baton entirely.

The Production EP and HOP now run this leg together, but with a clean split: the EP is client-facing, the HOP is studio-facing. The EP owns the relationship and creative trust. The HOP owns the internal engine — resourcing, job planning, system setup, and financial tracking.

This is the longest leg of the race — from project kickoff through final delivery. The Producer takes the baton for day-to-day client communication and project management, operating under the guidance of the EP and HOP when needed.

The HOP runs the studio-side engine throughout: overseeing budgets in Timmy from producers, managing producer workloads, identifying resourcing gaps, and making sure every internal system is functioning. BD EP is minimal here — available in the background if a senior relationship moment arises.

The Production EP's role
  • Remains the client's primary senior relationship and face of trust
  • Steps in for escalations, overages, and scope changes needing senior authority
  • Monitors delivery quality and client sentiment week to week
  • Is already thinking about the next job — looping back toward BD
The HOP's role
  • Manages producers day to day — guidance, problem-solving, and accountability
  • Runs the internal financial engine: Timmy, timesheets, budget tracking
  • Identifies when jobs are going off the rails and course-corrects early
  • Ensures quality, communication standards, and Versus process are upheld
The Producer's role
  • Owns day-to-day client communication and project management
  • Runs the project on the ground: schedules, reviews, feedback loops, and delivery
  • Escalates to EP or HOP when a situation needs senior handling
Team on this leg
Production EP Core
  • Senior client relationship, escalation point, delivery oversight
ECD ~90% of jobs
  • Creative direction, internal reviews, quality standard
HOP Core
  • Studio-side operational engine, producer management, financial tracking
Resource Manager / Coordinator Role in development
  • Will support resourcing plans and freelance tracking as role is built out
Producer Core
  • Day-to-day client comms, project management, execution
Artists Core
  • Motion, VFX, Design, Edit — the work itself
BD EP Minimal
  • Oversight as needed — available if client relationship needs senior attention
When this leg works well, clients never see the seam between the Producer, the EP, and the HOP. Each person knows their lane. Escalations are fast, decisions are clean, and delivery is never a surprise.

How this leg works

The final leg doesn't end at delivery — it completes the loop. Once a project ships, the work isn't over: it needs to be documented, positioned, and activated so Versus gets full value from what it built. Great work that nobody sees outside the studio is a missed opportunity.

Most marketing materials are created between Legs 1 and 4 — not assembled as an afterthought. For the roughly 20% of projects that need bespoke materials, the HOP identifies and resources the right team. But the goal is always to have assets ready before delivery, not chasing them after.

This leg feeds directly back to BD at the start line. Every case study is a future pitch, every award is a door opener, every social post is a conversation starter. The better this leg runs, the stronger Leg 1 becomes.

The BD EP's role
  • Leads the conversation with the client about what can be shared publicly
  • Gets sign-off on footage, credits, and any PR or social use
  • Identifies whether this project opens the door to a follow-up opportunity
The ECD's role
  • Ensures the work is framed in a way that advances the Versus creative brand
  • Drives award submissions and positions work for industry recognition
  • Signs off on how the studio's craft is represented externally
Team on this leg
BD EP Core
  • Client approval, PR sign-off, follow-up opportunity identification
ECD Core
  • Creative brand framing, award submissions, external representation
Production EP Minimal
  • Oversight as needed — available for high-value PR or relationship moments
HOP ~20% of projects
  • Identifies and resources the team when bespoke marketing assets are needed
Producer / Artists ~20% of projects
  • Create bespoke marketing assets when needed — ideally scoped and completed during Leg 3
The goal is for marketing asset creation to happen during Leg 3 — not after delivery. When Leg 4 has everything it needs before the finish line, the loop back to BD is faster, cleaner, and more effective.
